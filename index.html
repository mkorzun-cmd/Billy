About the book

A book that works on two levels at once

Billy has a brand new boat. And the boat has a friend.

Together they sail past the tall grass and out onto the open sea — through a storm that nearly swallows them whole, past a very confused octopus, and into an encounter with a mysterious purple sea creature who sneezes green sparkles and blows the sail clean away.

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea.

Each adventure damages a plank. Each plank is replaced with something new — cherry red, golden yellow, red and white, soft green. Slowly, lovingly, the boat becomes a patchwork of its own history.

Until one hazy afternoon, Billy looks down and sees the last original board — worn thin and smooth beneath his feet, loved all the way through. And he cannot bring himself to replace it.

Is it still the same boat?

Children will feel the answer. Adults will recognize it.

The pitch

Billy and his Beloved Boat is a picture book for ages 5–8 — and for every adult in the room. Built on the ancient philosophical thought experiment known as the Ship of Theseus, this book translates one of humanity's oldest questions into a story a child can feel in their chest before they can explain it with words.

Five adventures. Five repairs. A patchwork boat that becomes more itself with every plank replaced. And one worn board that Billy simply cannot bear to let go.

It is funny — CLUNK, CRACK, AHHHHH CHOOOOOO. It is tender — "It's alright," Billy said to the boat. "It's ok," he said to his someone. It is philosophical without ever being pretentious. And it ends with the only question worth asking.

Comparable titles: The Giving Tree (Silverstein), The Velveteen Rabbit (Williams), Last Stop on Market Street (de la Peña). Like these books, Billy and his Beloved Boat operates simultaneously for child and adult — the child experiences the adventure, the adult understands the meaning, and they meet in the conversation that follows.

A companion microsite with downloadable discussion guides, classroom resources, coloring pages, and conversation starters for multiple age levels will accompany the book at publication.
