Billy and his
Beloved Boat

The complete story

OpEning

Billy was a boy with a brand new boat.

And the boat was a boat with a friend.

He dreamed about the boat, he dreamed in the boat, he even dreamed standing beside the boat… and in every dream, they were out on the open sea.

The boat dreamed too. On sunny days when the water was still, the boat rocked — just a little — when Billy came near.

As Billy stepped onto the bow of the boat, he would lean in close and whisper, so quietly only the boat could hear:

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea.
The anchor

On a very special day, Billy and his boat sailed out past the tall grass and into the wide open sea.

Once they found a breezy blue spot, Billy dropped the anchor and he and the boat watched as it sank deep into the water below.

Billy and his boat spent the day there, bopping in the waves and making silly faces at a very confused octopus.

Billy was so busy being the best sailor who ever sailed that he didn't notice the day fading into dark.

Billy rushed to pull the anchor up so they could return home. As he pulled, the anchor rose up under the boat, then it was beside the boat, and finally he heaved it up into the boat when —

CLUNK.

The anchor crashed right through one of the boat's smooth new boards.

Billy looked at the hole. The hole looked back at Billy. The boat went very, very still.

Billy scratched his head. He wiggled his toe in the broken boards. Then he and the boat sailed home as the cool night air surrounded them.

That night, Billy found a new plank of cherry red wood, and careful as careful could be, he replaced the broken brown board with the new one.

When the last nail was in, Billy stepped onto the bow and leaned in close.

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea. The boat rocked — just a little.
The storm

One morning, when the sky was glowing pink, Billy and his boat sailed further than they had ever sailed before.

With telescope in hand, Billy scanned the horizon for pirates and distant lands hiding at the edge of the world.

The sky twisted from pink to a bruised gray. The waves growled and the wind began to howl. Thunder rumbled so deep and angry that Billy felt it in his teeth.

A storm swirled beneath the boat, then swelled beside the boat, and spilled into the boat.

And then —

CRACK.

A bolt of lightning split the sky and struck the boat's mast, shattering it to pieces.

The boat groaned. Billy gasped. And the storm hissed around them.

Billy held onto the boat. And the boat held onto Billy. Together, they battled through the swelling waves back to the shore.

That night, Billy found a long beam of golden yellow wood, and careful as careful could be, he replaced the broken mast with the new one.

When the last nail was in, Billy placed his hand on the mast and leaned in close.

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea. The boat's mast bowed — just a little.
The creature

One afternoon, Billy and his boat drifted into a quiet cove where the water was green and glassy and very, very deep.

Billy leaned over the edge of the boat, his nose nearly touching the water, and admired his reflection. The boat rested.

That is when he saw it.

First, two enormous eyes appeared deep in the water. Then a great shadow rose up under the boat, giant bubbles rose beside the boat, and then — with an enormous PLOOOP — a mysterious purple sea creature flopped inside the boat.

Before he could stop himself, Billy let out a round belly laugh that echoed through the cove. The boat bobbed. The creature laughed too, its long purple parts flopping everywhere.

And then...

Ah... Ahhhh... AHHHHH CHOOOOOO.

The creature sneezed — spraying Billy head to toe in something green and sparkly — and blowing the boat's sail clean off and away into the distance.

Billy stood there, dripping. The boat sat there, gaping. The creature froze.

"Gesundheit!" Billy said through a laugh.

The boat tilted its deck toward the water, and the creature slid back down into a swirl of bubbles.

That night, Billy found red and white fabric, and careful as careful could be, he replaced the missing sail with a new one.

When the last stitch was in, Billy hoisted the sail and leaned in close.

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea. The boat's sail waved — just a little.
The someone

Some summers later, Billy didn't sail alone anymore.

There was someone who fit just right beside him. Someone who fit the boat just right too. On the days the three of them sailed together, the boat sailed a little differently. Easier. Fuller.

One golden afternoon, as they settled back into the dock, Billy grabbed his someone by the hand and twirled them around the boat. They twirled outside the boat, and beside the boat, even inside the boat. The boat danced beneath them. Full of delight, his someone spun across the deck and right through a soft, worn board.

And with a soft snap, the boat's deck wore through.

Billy's someone knelt and touched the wounded board gently. Billy looked at his aging boat.

"It's alright," Billy said to the boat.
"It's ok," he said to his someone.

That evening, Billy and his someone sat together on the dock, passing tools back and forth without needing to ask. Billy found a plank of soft green wood. And together, careful as careful could be, they replaced the broken board with the new one.

When the last nail was in, Billy placed his hand on the boat and leaned in close.

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea. He felt someone's hand rest on top of his.
The boat settled — just a little deeper — into the water.
The worn plank

Many years later, Billy still sailed.

The boat was older now. So was Billy. Over the years, one by one, nearly every plank and beam and board had been replaced. The anchor. The hull. The mast. The sail. The deck. Each piece renewed, each piece a different color now — yellow and red and blue and green and gold — a patchwork of every adventure they had ever shared.

All except one.

In the very center of the bow, there remained one original board. Worn smooth and thin beneath Billy's feet. The board that had stood beneath him on the very first day, and every single day since. The board that knew the weight of him by heart.

One hazy afternoon, Billy looked down and saw that it had grown very thin. Not broken. Not cracked. Just loved all the way through.

He stood beside the plank. Then he knelt beside the plank. And then he eventually sat right on top of the plank.

That night, Billy didn't grab his tools. He didn't reach for a shiny new board. He ran his hand along the worn wood slowly and leaned in close.

Steady, my friend. It's just you and me, out on the sea. The boat didn't rock. It didn't bow. It didn't wave or settle.
It just sat right there with him.
And that was enough.
The ending

That night, Billy dreamed.

He dreamed about the boat, he dreamed of the storm that nearly swallowed the boat, he dreamed of the sea creature beneath the boat, he dreamed twirling his someone around the boat… and in every dream, they were out on the open sea.

And somewhere in the deep of the night, Billy smiled.

And his beloved boat smiled too.
